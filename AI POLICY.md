# AI-Generated Content and Scientific Validation Policy

Awesome Spectral Indices welcomes contributions developed using a wide range of methodologies, including traditional analytical approaches, data-driven methods, optimization techniques, and artificial intelligence (AI) systems.

The project does not evaluate submissions based on how an index was created. Instead, submissions are evaluated based on their scientific validity, reproducibility, and usefulness to the remote sensing community.

## AI-Assisted and AI-Generated Indices

The use of AI tools, including large language models (LLMs), machine learning algorithms, or autonomous agents, is not prohibited.

However, AI-generated formulas, hypotheses, or candidate indices are not considered scientific contributions by themselves.

A spectral index becomes a valid scientific contribution only when supported by appropriate scientific evidence.

## Scientific Validation Requirements

To be considered for inclusion, a submitted index should provide evidence of scientific validity, such as:

* A peer-reviewed publication describing the index.
* A technical report, thesis, or preprint containing experimental evaluation.
* Benchmark comparisons against relevant existing indices.
* Quantitative performance assessment on representative datasets.
* A clear description of the intended application and rationale.

The maintainers may request additional information when the available evidence is insufficient.

## Automated Submissions

Large-scale automated submissions may be rejected without review.

Examples include:

* Bulk submissions generated by scripts or autonomous agents.
* Multiple indices submitted simultaneously without supporting validation.
* Automatically generated repositories containing large numbers of untested formulas.

The existence of a repository, DOI, website, preprint, or other publication mechanism does not automatically imply scientific validity.

## Transparency Requirements

Contributors are encouraged to disclose when an index was generated or assisted by AI systems.

Such disclosure will not negatively affect the evaluation process.

However, failure to disclose substantial AI involvement may be considered during the review of a submission if concerns regarding validity arise.

## Curatorial Discretion

Awesome Spectral Indices is a curated community resource rather than an exhaustive catalogue of all proposed formulas.

The maintainers reserve the right to reject submissions that:

* Lack sufficient scientific evidence.
* Cannot be reasonably verified.
* Appear to be automatically generated without validation.
* Are likely to create confusion within the remote sensing community.

## Guiding Principle

The purpose of Awesome Spectral Indices is to facilitate the discovery and use of scientifically meaningful spectral indices.

The project welcomes innovation, including AI-assisted innovation, while maintaining the distinction between generated hypotheses and validated scientific knowledge.
